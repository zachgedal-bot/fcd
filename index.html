Football has evolved into a high-stakes asset class. We analyze the Football Club Database (FCD) reports to demonstrate how granular metrics, hidden attributes, and modeling determine the enterprise value of modern clubs and players.

1. The Foundation: Quantifying Performance

Traditional counting stats are often misleading. Modern valuation relies on Expected Goals (xG) and Goals Added (g+). "Hidden Attributes" like Professionalism can be major drivers of long-term development.

Efficiency Gap: Goals vs. xG

Comparing raw output against underlying chance quality.

Insight: Player B scored 18 goals from 12.2 xG. That can mean regression risk or elite finishing. You need deeper context to price it.

The "Hidden" Attributes

Psychological factors driving career outcomes.

Key Finding: Professionalism often correlates with staying near peak longer, which shows up as lower discounting in valuation.
